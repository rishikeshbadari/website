# Aperiphoria

I've noticed that staying indoors for long periods of time can be weirdly distressing. Even stepping outside for a few seconds can alleviate these feelings. 

For a while I thought this was due to fresh air or the walking that would usually accompany stepping outside, but recently, I've started to think it's due to the walls themselves.

After a few trips to the beach, I've become more confident in this theory. Something to the opposite effect of claustrophobia happens - you feel freer and more relaxed. 

This should be called "aperiphoria": *aperi-* (open) + *-phoria* (the state of being) - the state of being open.
